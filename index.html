Hi, my name is Ian.

I'm a web developer from Edmonton, Alberta, and I love handcrafting fluid web experiences.

I've been programming for nearly half my life now, and my philosophy has always been that the best digital experiences are the ones built with love, from the ground up.

People say to choose the right tool for the job, and what better tool is there than one custom-made for the job?

TeamSummon

TeamSummon was a natural evolution of my work at Protatomonster.

Looking for an opportunity to expand on YouTube success, TeamSummon was born.

In essence, an independent Team Builder, offering the same functionality Riot Games would eventually implement into the official League of Legends client.

With over 100,000 registered users in the first month, the RESTful Ajax API-driven service matched players with players of a similar skill level using a custom algorithm, utilizing player feedback to reward positive players

Protatomonster

I started work at Protatomonster as a volunteer in 2011.

After showing them my skills in a volunteer position, I was offered a paid position as their lead web applications developer.

Protatomonster grew rapidly, approaching a million subscribers on YouTube, and as their viewship grew, so did their need for a web presence.

I designed their new home on the web from the ground up, with CMS custom written to meet their video-heavy content needs.

With the success of their new website, Protatomonster was receiving more video submissions than ever.

Hence, a heavy-duty video management application that would be used to receive all new replays, catalogue them, then delegate sorting to the review staff, all the while giving the submitter a tracking code that they can use to view the progress like you would your mail.

To this day, almost 100,000 replays have gone through this process
